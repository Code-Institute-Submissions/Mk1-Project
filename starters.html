Torched mackerel, blood orange & puffed grains

This dish is deep but fantasticly fresh, and if you are in the mood it can be made turned into a summer salad and had as a main course. We recommend fresh caught mackrel (we love the handline caught cornish coast mackrel here at ginger nuts) but dont worry if you need to substitue it at home you will still end up with a fantastic dish.

1h
Prep
10m
Cook
1h10
Total
Ingredients Method
  • Mackrel Cure Batch
    • 74% Cornish Salt
    • 22% Caster Sugar
    • 2% Celery Salt
    • 1% Citrus Fruit Zest
    • 1% Deseeded Red Chilli
  • Whole line caught Mackrel(or fresh mackrel fillets)
  • Wild Rice (or puffed wild rice
  • Blood Orange
  • Castel franco
  • Cold pressed rapeseed oil
  • Mackrel
    • Mix all cure ingredients together with whisk
    • Fillet Mackrel and score the skin with equal finger spacing
    • In a container sprinkle a layer or cure mix then place fillets in and cover with cure mix
    • Leave mackrel for 1 hour (or overnight if you want to have this dish cold)
    • Rinse the cure off the mackrel. Then place in zip lock bag, seal and poach on a low simmer for 4mins
    • Remove mackrel and pat dry blow tourch to serve
  • Puffed Rice
    • Heat pan of neutral oil to 210c
    • Take wild rice and lower in to oil using a sieve
    • Once riced has finished puffing remove from oil and place on dry cloth.
  • Salad
    • Wash castel franco and tear into small pieces
    • Segment Blood oranges
    • Juice remainder of oranges and whisk together with cold pressed rapeseed with a pinch of salt
  • Putting it together
    • Toss Salad with dressing and add to base of plate, sprinkle with genourus helping of puffed rice
    • Blow touch your mackrel and place on top of salad
    • Garnish plate with a drizzle of dressing over the top.
